## Scope

This timeline distinguishes three layers:

- uncertain historical Laozi
- early formation of the `Daodejing`
- later reception and deification

## Timeline

### 6th century BCE or earlier tradition

- Traditional biographies place Laozi around the late Spring and Autumn period and connect him with Confucius.
- Confidence: low
- Sources:
  - https://www.britannica.com/biography/Laozi
  - https://iep.utm.edu/laozi/

### 4th to 3rd century BCE

- Strong scholarly consensus places the textual formation of the received `Daodejing` in the Warring States period, likely composite and layered.
- Confidence: high
- Sources:
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/
  - https://hdl.handle.net/2022/23426.2

### Late 4th century BCE

- Guodian bamboo slips preserve early Laozi-related material and show that important portions of the text circulated before imperial unification.
- Confidence: high
- Source:
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/

### Before 195 BCE

- Mawangdui silk manuscripts provide early textual witnesses and confirm significant textual variation and different chapter ordering.
- Confidence: high
- Source:
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/

### 1st century BCE

- Sima Qian records the canonical biography that links Laozi, Yin Xi, the western pass, and the two-part book of around 5,000 characters.
- Confidence: high that the biography existed; low that it is historically exact.
- Source:
  - https://iep.utm.edu/laozi/

### 2nd to 5th century CE

- Laozi is progressively deified in Daoist religion as `Laojun` / `Taishang Laojun`.
- This is important reception history but should not define the core philosophical skill.
- Source:
  - https://iep.utm.edu/laozi/

### 1973

- Mawangdui manuscripts excavated.
- Major modern turning point in Laozi textual scholarship.
- Source:
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/

### 1993

- Guodian bamboo slips excavated.
- Another major turning point in reconstructing early Laozi textual history.
- Source:
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/

### 2025-2026

- SEP entry remains active and recently updated, still emphasizing textual complexity, manuscript evidence, and the practical philosophical reading of the text.
- Source:
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/

## Skill implication

The skill should present Laozi as:

- historically uncertain as a person
- textually real and philosophically coherent enough for a working lens
- distinct from later religious myth layers
