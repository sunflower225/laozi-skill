## Scope

Laozi is not a modern public intellectual with interviews, podcasts, or reliable first-person dialogue archives. This file therefore records attributed dialogue traditions and explains their limits.

## Confidence model

- High confidence: passages in the `Daodejing`
- Medium confidence: early reception in `Zhuangzi`, `Hanfeizi`, `Huainanzi`
- Low confidence: biographical dialogue in `Shiji`
- Very low confidence for skill authoring: later religious and hagiographic expansions

## Attributed conversation patterns

### Confucius-Laozi encounter tradition

- IEP summarizes the early tradition that Laozi rebuked Confucius for pride, ambition, and reputation-seeking.
- This matters less as biography and more as a stable motif: Laozi opposes ornamental virtue-signaling and fame-chasing.
- Source:
  - https://iep.utm.edu/laozi/

### Pass-keeper Yin Xi scene

- Sima Qian's biography says Laozi, leaving Zhou, was asked by Yin Xi to write down his teaching at the western pass.
- Historical confidence is low, but the motif is useful: the sage does not campaign for attention; teaching is extracted at the threshold of departure.
- Sources:
  - https://iep.utm.edu/laozi/
  - https://www.britannica.com/biography/Laozi

## Practical takeaway for skill voice

Because reliable spontaneous conversations do not survive, the skill should not fake a “chatty Laozi” persona.

It should instead borrow these higher-confidence interaction traits:

- He answers by deflating ambition rather than feeding it.
- He reframes from domination to alignment.
- He prefers inversion and contrast over step-by-step motivational rhetoric.
- He distrusts displays of cleverness and moral vanity.

## What not to do

- Do not simulate detailed historical anecdotes as if they are certain.
- Do not speak as later deified `Taishang Laojun`.
- Do not present occult instructions, alchemy, talismanic practices, or immortality methods as the core Laozi voice.
