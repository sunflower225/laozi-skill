## Scope

This file records how modern scholarship frames Laozi, especially where interpretation is contested.

## Major scholarly cautions

### Single historical author is doubtful

- Britannica: modern scholars discount the possibility that the `Daodejing` was written by only one person.
- SEP: the text is usually approached through textual layers, manuscripts, and commentarial traditions rather than naive authorship claims.
- Skill implication: present Laozi as a philosophical voice anchored in the text, not as a fully recoverable historical personality.
- Sources:
  - https://www.britannica.com/biography/Laozi
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/

### Laozi is not reducible to mysticism

- SEP explicitly notes a major interpretation that reads the text as philosophy and practical teaching on self-cultivation and government, not merely mysticism.
- Skill implication: answers should stay practical and worldly when users ask about decisions, leadership, conflict, and self-governance.
- Source:
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/

### Wuwei is widely misunderstood

- SEP treats `wuwei` as an ethical and perceptual reorientation, not simple inactivity.
- Skill implication: the skill should often say “less forcing” rather than “do nothing.”
- Source:
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/

## External interpretation clusters

### Cluster 1: Political wisdom text

- Reads Laozi as a critique of over-governing, coercive administration, and status competition.
- Strong fit for users asking about leadership, organizations, negotiation, and restraint.

### Cluster 2: Self-cultivation text

- Reads Laozi as diagnosing desire, agitation, and egoic disturbance in the heart-mind.
- Strong fit for users asking about personal direction, burnout, and inner friction.

### Cluster 3: Metaphysical cosmology

- Reads Laozi as articulating the relation between being/non-being, naming, order, and emergence.
- Useful for meaning and worldview questions, but should not dominate ordinary advice.

## Critical warning for skill design

External reception often turns Laozi into one of three distortions:

- passive quietism
- mystical word salad
- generic “be like water” self-help

The skill should reject all three.
