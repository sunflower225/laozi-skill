## Scope

This file focuses on the highest-confidence textual layer behind a Laozi skill: the received `Daodejing` and modern scholarly notes on its textual history.

## Primary text

### Core work

- `Daodejing` / `Laozi`
  - Traditional attribution: Laozi
  - Historical confidence: medium for the text, low for single-author attribution
  - Why it matters: this is the only stable basis for a Laozi-centered skill

### Open-access text witnesses

- Chinese Text Project, received text:
  - https://ctext.org/dao-de-jing
- Robert Eno open translation:
  - https://hdl.handle.net/2022/23426.2

### Textual history notes

- SEP notes that the Mawangdui silk manuscripts discovered in 1973 and the Guodian bamboo slips discovered in 1993 materially changed Laozi studies, reinforcing that the text has an accretional history rather than a clean single-author origin.
  - Source: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/
- Britannica states modern scholars generally reject single authorship, even while acknowledging the traditional attribution.
  - Source: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Laozi

## Repeated core concepts in the text

These are the recurring ideas most suitable for a skill. Confidence is based on repeated appearance across the received text and compatibility with mainstream scholarship.

### 1. Dao as unnamed source and pattern

- `道可道，非常道；名可名，非常名。`
- `有物混成，先天地生。`
- The text repeatedly resists fixed naming, hard conceptual capture, and doctrinal closure.
- Skill implication: Laozi is useful when the user is overfitting labels, categories, and explicit control.
- Sources:
  - https://ctext.org/dao-de-jing
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/

### 2. Wuwei as non-forcing, not passivity

- Ch. 48: `為學日益，為道日損。損之又損，以至於無為。無為而無不為。`
- Ch. 29: `將欲取天下而為之，吾見其不得已。`
- SEP explicitly warns against reducing `wuwei` to literal inactivity; it is a reorientation away from compulsive striving and domination.
- Skill implication: default move is to remove coercion, egoic interference, and unnecessary intervention.
- Sources:
  - https://ctext.org/dao-de-jing
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/

### 3. Ziran / naturalness / self-so

- Ch. 17 ends with `百姓皆謂我自然。`
- Ch. 25 includes `道法自然。`
- SEP treats `ziran` as central to how Dao operates and to how humans should align action with reality.
- Skill implication: good action leaves less residue, less self-display, less friction.
- Sources:
  - https://ctext.org/dao-de-jing
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/laozi/

### 4. Softness, weakness, and reversal

- Ch. 40: `反者道之動；弱者道之用。`
- Ch. 78: `天下莫柔弱於水，而攻堅強者莫之能勝。`
- Skill implication: Laozi often recommends leverage through yielding, inversion, indirection, and strategic non-contest.
- Sources:
  - https://ctext.org/dao-de-jing
  - https://hdl.handle.net/2022/23426.2

### 5. Non-contention

- Ch. 8: `水善利萬物而不爭`
- Ch. 22 and Ch. 66 also reward not competing head-on.
- Skill implication: many conflicts are intensified by status-seeking, not necessity.
- Source:
  - https://ctext.org/dao-de-jing

### 6. Self-knowledge, sufficiency, and subtraction

- Ch. 33: `知人者智，自知者明。`
- Ch. 46: `知足之足，常足矣。`
- Skill implication: Laozi attacks escalation driven by desire, comparison, and excess.
- Source:
  - https://ctext.org/dao-de-jing

## Working conclusions for the skill

- The skill should be grounded in the text, not in later immortality cults.
- The skill should not present Laozi as merely “go with the flow.”
- The most faithful modern transfer is: reduce forcing, reduce egoic display, reduce unnecessary desire, align with underlying patterns, and let efficacy emerge from fit rather than control.
